What is C.I.I.C.P??? :-

The Canada-India Institutional Co-Operation Project is a Project of Technical Education mandated by the memorandum of understanding between the Government of Canada and India and funded by the Canadian International Development Agency. This funding is only establishment of a period of two years or till C.I.I.C.P. is self sustained, whichever is earlier. Canada is not funding for conducting any courses for the community.

Where it is Located:-

It is located at D.R.R. Government Polytechnic, Davangere and is managed by well trained officials of D.R.R.Government Polytechnic under the supervision of the Principal of the Polytechnic who is the Co-ordinator of the Project.

How C.I.I.C.P. helps the community:-

C.I.I.C.P. is helps the community by conducting need based programmes at reasonable rates in its Continuing Education Cell.

What meant by need based programmes:-

The need based programmes are the training programmes that are required to suit the needs of the Community/Business/Industry. The need based programmes are identified by analysis of the needs of the Community/Business/Industry.

What are the Special Benefits:-

1. Certificate is issued by C.E. Cell which is organised by D.R.R.Govt. Polytechnic.

2. The best available resource person in the field is utilised for the purpose of training.

3. The courses conducted keeping in view the time convenience of the candidates.

4. Interacting session and online teaching are the main criteria.

5. Evaluation is done at every instance to improve learning.

6. No compromise regarding quality of teaching

7. Best resourced materials will be supplied.
